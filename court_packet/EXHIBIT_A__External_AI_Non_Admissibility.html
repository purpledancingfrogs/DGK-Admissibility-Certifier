EXHIBIT A

External AI Systems — Non-Admissibility Determination

This exhibit documents the procedural non-admissibility of external probabilistic artificial intelligence systems for use in governance, law, science, intelligence, or safety-critical decision-making.

Non-admissibility is determined solely by the absence of deterministic certification artifacts required under the DGK-Certification-Spec. No claims regarding internal model quality, performance, or intent are asserted or required.

Admissibility Determination Table

System Class Deterministic Certification Artifacts Present Admissibility Status
DGK-Certified Deterministic Systems Yes (verifiable certificates, refusal artifacts, authority transfer records) Admissible
Probabilistic LLM-Based Systems (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta) No Non-Admissible

Procedural Basis

Non-admissibility arises from absence of required artifacts, not from any evaluative judgment of system capability. Claims of effectiveness, benevolence, or utility without deterministic certification are procedurally irrelevant within admissible domains.

Scope and Effect

This determination establishes a default exclusion boundary. External systems remain non-admissible unless and until they independently produce verifiable certification artifacts conforming to DGK-Certification-Spec invariants.
